Introduction

Mechatronics can be classified as the integration of mechanics, electronics, computing, and controls in order to improve efficiency and functionality of designs and systems. A mechatronic system is therefore an amalgamation of these aspects, in which it takes in signals from the environment and processes them to generate output signals through a transformation of motion or computation. Mechatronic systems make up a significant part of everyday life, often without users realizing just how important they are. Within one’s home, there are countless examples of mechatronics at work that make tasks more efficient. In the fast-paced environment made possible by technological advancements, it can be easy to forget simple duties like sweeping the floors, watering plants, or cleaning up after pets, which is why machines and systems have been created to complete these tasks automatically. There are many examples of systems designed to automatically water plants that use different system designs to accomplish this task. Some rely solely on a timed release of a given amount of water, while others sense environmental levels and adjust the watering amount and interval to the specific plant. For this project, we propose to design a watering system incorporating a variety of sensors to accurately read environmental signals like moisture level, temperature, humidity, and positioning of an actuated robot that will dispense water as needed to a houseplant. This system would allow the user to worry less about over- or under-watering a plant and could extend plant longevity.
